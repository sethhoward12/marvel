Black Panther

King of Wakanda

This movie is set in Marvel's own Wakanda, Africa. It is a highly advanced society that is protected and ruled by one king. T'Challa, portrayed by the late Chadwick Boseman, takes the place of King after his father, T'Chaka, passes away.

Conflict arises as T'Challa must fight to keep his rights as king, and to protect his people from being attacked.

One of Wakanda's most valuable resources is a metallic material known as Vibranium. It is able to absorb sound waves, vibrations, as well as kinetic energy. It is the main resource that powers Wakanda and fuels their technology.

The antagonists of the story seek to steal the Vibranium and sell it. T'Challa eventually partners with Agent Phil, a member of S.H.I.E.L.D.

To be the Black Panther is simply not just a title of being king, it is a symbol of power and leadership. The Black Panther's powers are acquired via consuming a heart-shaped herb which grants the consumer great strength, stamina, and agility. The process follows several rituals, including that the individual being the Black Panther will enter another dimension to walk with their ancestors. T'Challa goes through the rituals several times throughout the film, each one showing how physically and mentally exhausting the process is.

Impact of Black Panther

One of the biggest things that made this movie stand out was having the first black lead actor to be a super hero in a big budget marvel movie. Prior to this movie there had really only been black side actors in this series rather than being the main hero. This has such a big impact around the world because the most popular super hero movies of the last 20 years had only revolved around white people and esspecially kids who are black never got to see a super hero that looked like them on the big screen. Since Black Panther released it has opened up a bunch of new opportunities for non white actors to be the lead super hero in marvel movies.
